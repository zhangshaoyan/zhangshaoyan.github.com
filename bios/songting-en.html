SONG Ting - Director of Photography

Song Ting graduated from University of International Business & Economics in 2007, double majoring in finance and English literature. After graduation, Song Ting joined PricewaterhouseCoopers China and worked as associate auditor for one year.

Ting started her career as a filmmaker in 2008. Her working experiences include producer, line producer, writer, director, and director of photography. Ting joined New York University’s graduate film program in 2009, and focused on cinematography after entering school. During her study at NYU, Ting has worked (as DoP, 2nd Unit DoP, or camera operator) for 6 features and 18 short films (including commercials and music videos) in different places all over the world.

Ting’s own short film "Kinfe and Kinfe" was officlally selected into 2012 Los Angeles Asian American Film Festival, "My Favorite Crane" was selected into NYU "East Meet West" Screening; short film "Nindy" which she produced was officially selected into 2011 Vancouver International Film Festival; short film "The Keeper" which she DPed was an official project of 2011 Asian Film Academy, short film "Café Night" was an official project of 2011 Golden Horse Film Academy; short film "Bye, Bye Brother" was an official project of Singapore Art Museum short film program.
