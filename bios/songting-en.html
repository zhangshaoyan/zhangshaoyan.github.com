SONG Ting - Director of Photography

Song Ting graduated from University of International Business & Economics in 2007, holding a joint degree of finance and English lterature. After graduation, Song Ting joined PricewaterhouseCoopers China, worked as associate auditor for one year.

Ting officially started her career as a filmmaker in 2008. So far she has worked in different positions including producer, line producer, writer, director, director of photography. She joined New York University’s graduate film program in 2009, and focused on cinematography. During her study at NYU, Ting has worked on 6 features and 18 short films (including commercials and music videos) in various places like Mainland China, U.S.A, Hong Kong, Singapore, Taiwan, South Korea, etc.

Ting’s own short film “Kinfe and Kinfe” was officlally selected into 2012 Los Angeles Asian American Film Festival, “My Favorite Crane” was selected into NYU “East Meet West” Screening; short film “Nindy” which she produced was officially selected into 2011 Vancouver International Film Festival. Short film “The Keeper” which she worked as a DoP was an official project of 2011 Asian Film Academy, short film “Café Night” which she worked as a DoP was an official project of 2011 Golden Horse Film Academy; short film “Bye, Bye Brother” which she worked as a DoP was an official project of Singapore Art Museum short film program.
